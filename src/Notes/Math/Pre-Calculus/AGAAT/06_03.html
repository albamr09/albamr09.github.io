Solution of Linear Systems by Row Transformations

Solving linear systems of equations can be streamlined by using matrices. Consider a system of three equations and three unknowns.

\begin{align} a_1 x + b_1y + c_1z = d_1 a_2 x + b_2y + c_2z = d_2 a_3 x + b_3y + c_3z = d_3 \end{align}

can be written as the following augmented matrix:

\begin{align} \begin{bmatrix} a_1 & b_1 & c_1 & d_1 \\ a_2 & b_2 & c_2 & d_2 \\ a_3 & b_3 & c_3 & d_3 \\ \end{bmatrix} \end{align}

Matrix Row Transformations

For any augmented matrix of a system of linear equations, the following row transformations will result in the matrix of an equivalent system.

  1. Any two rows may be interchanged.
  2. The elements of any row may be multiplied by a nonzero real number.
  3. Any row may be changed by adding to its elements a multiple of the corresponding elements of another row.
